# Campaign Frameworks by Type

Each framework below defines the architecture for a specific campaign type.
Use the Campaign Type Decision Tree in SKILL.md to select the right one.

---

## Demand Gen

**Goal:** Generate qualified pipeline (MQLs, SQLs, opportunities).

**Timeline:** 6-12 weeks per campaign cycle. First 2 weeks = setup + test.
Weeks 3-10 = scale what works. Weeks 11-12 = optimize and report.

**Channel Mix (typical priority order):**
1. Paid search (captures existing intent)
2. LinkedIn ads (B2B) or Meta ads (B2C/D2C)
3. Email nurture sequences (for captured leads)
4. Content/SEO (long-tail, compounds over time)
5. Webinars or events (high-intent conversion)

**Budget Allocation:**
- 40% paid channels (search + social)
- 25% content production (landing pages, lead magnets, blog)
- 20% email/nurture infrastructure
- 15% testing and experimental

**Success Metrics:**
- Primary: Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs), Cost per MQL
- Secondary: SQL conversion rate, pipeline value generated
- Tracking: CTR, landing page conversion rate (diagnostic only)

**Kill Criteria:**
- CPA > 2x target after 500 clicks on any channel --> pause that channel
- MQL-to-SQL conversion < 5% after 30 days --> re-evaluate lead quality/scoring
- Overall campaign ROI negative after full cycle --> post-mortem, do not repeat

## Launch

**Goal:** Maximum awareness and adoption of a new product, feature, or offering
within a compressed timeframe.

**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks. Pre-launch (2-3 weeks), launch week, post-launch
sustain (2-4 weeks).

**Phase Breakdown:**

*Pre-Launch (Weeks 1-3):*
- Tease content: social posts, email previews, waitlist
- Influencer/partner seeding
- Press/media outreach
- Internal alignment: sales enablement, support docs

*Launch Week:*
- Coordinated multi-channel push (email, social, paid, PR)
- Live event or webinar (optional but high-impact)
- Customer testimonials or case studies ready to deploy

*Post-Launch (Weeks 4-8):*
- Retargeting campaigns for launch-week visitors
- Email sequences for non-converters
- Social proof amplification (reviews, testimonials, user content)

**Budget Allocation:**
- 50% launch week concentrated spend
- 25% pre-launch seeding
- 25% post-launch sustain

**Success Metrics:**
- Primary: Sign-ups, activations, or first purchases (within 30 days)
- Secondary: Media coverage, social mentions, waitlist size
- Vanity guard: Do NOT report impressions as the primary metric

**Kill Criteria:**
- Pre-launch signup rate < 50% of target by week 2 --> extend pre-launch or
  adjust positioning
- Launch week conversion < 1% of reach --> messaging problem, pause paid spend,
  retest messaging

## Retention

**Goal:** Reduce churn, increase customer lifetime value (LTV), drive expansion
revenue.

**Timeline:** Ongoing, measured in 90-day cycles.

**Channel Mix:**
1. Email (lifecycle sequences, product tips, usage nudges)
2. In-app messaging (feature adoption, milestone celebrations)
3. Community (forums, user groups, events)
4. Customer success touchpoints (QBRs, health checks)

**Budget Allocation:**
- 40% email/lifecycle automation
- 25% in-app messaging and product tours
- 20% community and events
- 15% customer success tooling

**Success Metrics:**
- Primary: Net Revenue Retention (NRR), churn rate
- Secondary: Product adoption scores, NPS trend
- Leading indicators: Login frequency, feature usage, support ticket volume

**Key Segments:**
- At-risk (declining usage) --> re-engagement sequence
- Power users --> advocacy and referral programs
- New customers (0-90 days) --> onboarding optimization

**Kill Criteria:**
- Churn rate unchanged after 90 days --> re-evaluate segmentation and messaging
- Re-engagement sequence < 5% reactivation --> test new incentive or channel

## Awareness

**Goal:** Increase brand or category recognition among a defined target audience.

**Timeline:** 3-6 months minimum. Awareness compounds; short campaigns underperform.

**Channel Mix:**
1. Content marketing / thought leadership (blog, LinkedIn, podcasts)
2. Paid social (broad targeting, optimized for reach)
3. PR and media relations
4. Events / sponsorships
5. Partnerships / co-marketing

**Budget Allocation:**
- 35% content production and distribution
- 30% paid social (reach-optimized)
- 20% PR and events
- 15% partnerships

**Success Metrics:**
- Primary: Unaided brand recall (measured via survey), share of voice
- Secondary: Direct traffic growth, branded search volume
- Vanity guard: Social followers and impressions are NOT primary metrics

**Kill Criteria:**
- No measurable share-of-voice increase after 3 months --> re-evaluate
  audience targeting and channel mix
- Content engagement declining month-over-month for 2+ months --> refresh
  content themes

## Win-Back

**Goal:** Re-engage churned or lapsed customers/users.

**Timeline:** 4-6 week campaign, then measure for 30 days post-campaign.

**Channel Mix:**
1. Email (primary -- you already have their address)
2. Retargeting ads (second touch)
3. Direct outreach (high-value accounts only)

**Sequence Structure:**
- Day 0: "We miss you" + product update summary
- Day 3: Value reminder + incentive (discount, extended trial, new feature)
- Day 7: Social proof (what peers are achieving)
- Day 14: Final offer + urgency (offer expires)
- Day 21+: Move to low-frequency nurture or suppress

**Budget Allocation:**
- 50% email (near-zero marginal cost)
- 30% retargeting ads
- 20% direct outreach / incentives

**Success Metrics:**
- Primary: Reactivation rate (% of lapsed who return and complete an action)
- Secondary: Reactivated customer retention at 30/60/90 days
- Cost metric: Cost per reactivation vs cost of new acquisition

**Kill Criteria:**
- Reactivation rate < 3% after full sequence --> audience is truly churned,
  stop spending
- Reactivated users churn again within 30 days at > 50% --> product problem,
  not marketing problem

## Experiment

**Goal:** Validate a hypothesis about a new channel, message, audience, or offer
with minimum viable spend.

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks. Strict timebox -- experiments that run forever are not
experiments.

**Structure:**
1. Hypothesis: "We believe [action] will produce [outcome] for [audience]"
2. Minimum viable test: smallest budget/audience to get statistical significance
3. Success threshold: defined before launch (e.g., "CPA < $X" or "CTR > Y%")
4. Decision: Scale, iterate, or kill

**Budget:** Cap at 10% of total campaign budget. Never more.

**Success Metrics:**
- Whatever the hypothesis specifies -- no moving the goalposts after launch
- Statistical significance requirement: 95% confidence minimum

**Kill Criteria:**
- Results clearly negative at 50% of budget spent --> stop early, save budget
- Results inconclusive after full budget --> log learnings, do not scale

**Rules:**
- Change ONE variable at a time. Multi-variable tests require proper multivariate
  design.
- Document everything. An experiment without documentation is a waste of money.
- Negative results are valuable. Record why it failed and share with the team.

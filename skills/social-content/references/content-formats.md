# Content Formats Reference

## LinkedIn Post Templates

### The Story Arc
```
[Hook: Surprising outcome or confession -- 1 line that stops scrolling]

[Scene-setting: When/where, 1-2 sentences max]

[The tension: What went wrong or what was at stake]

[The pivot: What changed your thinking]

[The result: Specific, measurable if possible]

[The transferable lesson: What the READER can apply]

[Engagement close: Specific question, not "Thoughts?"]
```
**When to use:** Building trust, humanizing a brand, sharing hard-won lessons. Story posts get 2-3x comments vs. informational posts because they trigger emotional responses.

### The Contrarian Take
```
[Unpopular opinion stated as a bold declaration -- no hedging]

Here's what I mean:

[Reason 1 with evidence or personal experience]

[Reason 2 -- the strongest argument]

[Reason 3 -- the emotional/philosophical argument]

What I recommend instead:
[Your alternative, stated concretely]

[Invitation to disagree: "Tell me why I'm wrong" or "Where does this break down?"]
```
**When to use:** Establishing thought leadership, generating debate. Contrarian posts get high comment volume but must be defensible -- flimsy hot takes damage credibility.

### The Listicle
```
[X] things I learned about [topic] after [credibility signal]:

1. [Insight] -- [1-sentence explanation]

2. [Insight] -- [1-sentence explanation]

3. [Insight] -- [1-sentence explanation]

...

[Closing insight that ties the list together]

Which one would you add to this list?
```
**When to use:** Sharing frameworks, lessons, or curated knowledge. Works best with 5-10 items. Under 5 feels thin; over 10 loses readers.

### The Carousel (Document Post)
```
Slide 1: [Bold headline that promises specific value]
         [Subtitle: "A framework for..." or "[X] steps to..."]

Slides 2-3: [The problem / why this matters]

Slides 4-8: [One key point per slide, large text, minimal design]
            [Each slide: Headline + 2-3 bullet points OR 1 sentence]

Slide 9: [Summary / recap of key points]

Slide 10: [CTA: Follow for more, comment your thoughts, link mention]
          [Your name/handle/brand]
```
**Design rules:**
- Font size: 24pt minimum (must be readable on mobile without zooming)
- One color palette throughout (2-3 colors max)
- No walls of text -- if a slide has more than 40 words, split it
- Use icons/illustrations sparingly -- text-heavy carousels outperform image-heavy ones on LinkedIn

### The How-To
```
How to [specific outcome] in [timeframe/steps]:

Step 1: [Action verb + specific instruction]
- [Why this matters or what most people get wrong here]

Step 2: [Action verb + specific instruction]
- [Key detail that makes this actionable]

Step 3: [Action verb + specific instruction]
- [Common mistake to avoid at this step]

[Expected result if they follow these steps]

[CTA: "What step are you stuck on?" or "Save this for when you need it"]
```
**When to use:** Demonstrating expertise, creating saveable content. How-tos get high save rates, which signals quality to the algorithm.

## Twitter/X Thread Structures

### The Tutorial Thread
```
Tweet 1: [Hook + value promise]
"Here's exactly how to [outcome] (step-by-step):"

Tweet 2: Step 1 -- [action with enough detail to execute]

Tweet 3: Step 2 -- [action with specific example]

Tweet 4-6: Steps 3-5 -- [continue pattern]

Tweet 7: [Common mistakes to avoid when doing this]

Tweet 8: [Recap: "To summarize: 1... 2... 3..."]

Tweet 9: "If this helped, RT tweet 1 so others find it.
Follow @handle for more [topic]."
```

### The Story Thread
```
Tweet 1: [Intriguing hook -- tease the outcome, don't reveal it]
"[Time] ago, [unexpected thing happened]. Here's the full story:"

Tweet 2: [Set the scene -- who, what, where]

Tweet 3-5: [Rising action -- build tension with each tweet]

Tweet 6: [The turning point -- what changed everything]

Tweet 7: [The resolution and specific result]

Tweet 8: [The lesson -- make it transferable to the reader]

Tweet 9: "If you enjoyed this story, follow for more [topic].
Like/RT if someone you know needs to hear this."
```

### The Breakdown Thread
```
Tweet 1: "[Company/person] just [did remarkable thing].
Here's why it's genius (and what you can steal):"

Tweet 2: [Context -- what happened and why it matters]

Tweet 3-5: [Analysis point + insight per tweet]

Tweet 6: [The non-obvious lesson most people miss]

Tweet 7: [How to apply this to YOUR business/work]

Tweet 8: "TL;DR: [3-bullet summary]

Follow @handle for breakdowns like this every week."
```

### Rules for All Threads
- Tweet 1 MUST work as a standalone tweet -- it's the only one most people see
- Each tweet should be self-contained (some people read threads out of order in timelines)
- Use numbering sparingly -- "1/" looks formulaic. Let the content flow.
- Include an image or visual in tweet 1 to boost initial engagement

## Instagram Carousel Frameworks

### Educational Carousel (10 Slides)
```
Slide 1: Bold claim or question (this is your hook -- it must stop the scroll)
         Large text, high contrast, minimal design

Slide 2: "Here's what most people get wrong about [topic]:" (problem framing)

Slide 3-8: One point per slide
           Each slide: Bold headline + 1-2 supporting sentences
           Alternating background colors for visual rhythm

Slide 9: "Quick recap:" + bullet summary of all points

Slide 10: CTA slide
          "Save this for later"
          "Follow @handle for more [topic]"
          "Share this with someone who needs it"
```

### Before/After Carousel
```
Slide 1: "Stop doing [bad thing]. Start doing [good thing]."

Slides 2-9 (alternating):
  Even slides: "Instead of: [bad example]"
  Odd slides: "Try this: [good example]"

Slide 10: CTA + summary
```

### Myth-Busting Carousel
```
Slide 1: "[X] [topic] myths that are costing you [outcome]"

Slides 2-9: One myth per pair
  "MYTH: [common belief]"
  "TRUTH: [correct information + why it matters]"

Slide 10: "Which myth surprised you most? Comment below."
```

## TikTok / Reels Script Templates

### The Quick Tip (15-30 seconds)
```
[0-2 sec] HOOK: Text overlay with bold claim + direct eye contact
          "The one thing killing your [topic]..."

[2-8 sec] SETUP: Quick context -- who this is for and why it matters
          "If you're a [audience] trying to [goal], you're probably doing [wrong thing]."

[8-25 sec] VALUE: The actual tip/insight delivered clearly
           "Instead, try [specific action]. Here's why it works: [brief explanation]."

[25-30 sec] CTA: "Follow for more [topic] tips" or "Save this for later"
```

### The Story Format (30-60 seconds)
```
[0-2 sec] HOOK: "So this happened to me last week..."
          (Start mid-story, never with a greeting)

[2-15 sec] RISING ACTION: Build the narrative quickly
           Use text overlays for key details

[15-25 sec] THE TURN: The surprising moment or revelation

[25-45 sec] THE LESSON: What you learned and what the viewer should take away

[45-55 sec] PROOF/RESULT: Show the outcome if possible

[55-60 sec] CTA: "Has this happened to you? Comment below"
```

### The List Format (15-45 seconds)
```
[0-2 sec] HOOK: "[Number] [things] you need to [outcome] in [year]"
          Text overlay + counting gesture or visual

[2-35 sec] RAPID FIRE: Each point gets 3-5 seconds
           Use text overlays for each point
           Match audio rhythm to point transitions

[35-45 sec] WRAP: "Which one are you trying first? Comment the number."
```

## Content Calendar Templates

### Weekly Calendar (Single Platform Focus)
| Day | Content Type | Topic Source | Format |
|-----|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Monday | Educational | Pillar 1 | Text post / Carousel |
| Tuesday | Engagement | Community question or poll | Short post |
| Wednesday | Value | Pillar 2 | Thread / Carousel / Video |
| Thursday | Story | Personal experience | Text post / Reel |
| Friday | Hot take or opinion | Industry news | Text post |
| Weekend | Optional: casual / behind-scenes | Personal brand | Story / short post |

### Weekly Calendar (Multi-Platform)
| Day | LinkedIn | Twitter/X | Instagram |
|-----|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Monday | Industry insight (text) | Thread (from same topic) | Carousel (visual version) |
| Tuesday | -- | Engagement + replies | Stories (BTS) |
| Wednesday | Educational how-to | Tips thread | Reel (quick tip) |
| Thursday | Story post | -- | Educational carousel |
| Friday | Contrarian take | Engagement + hot take | Stories (poll/question) |

### Content Pillar Distribution
Allocate your content across 4-5 pillars:

| Pillar | % of Posts | Purpose | Example Topics |
|--------|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Expertise/Education | 35% | Demonstrate knowledge, earn saves | How-tos, frameworks, lessons |
| Stories/Personal | 25% | Build trust, earn comments | Failures, wins, journey |
| Industry Commentary | 20% | Stay relevant, earn shares | Trends, news analysis, predictions |
| Community/Engagement | 15% | Build relationships, earn replies | Questions, polls, shoutouts |
| Promotional | 5% | Drive business results | Product, offers, case studies |

**The 5% promotion rule is critical.** If more than 10% of your content is promotional, engagement rates drop across ALL your content because the algorithm learns your account produces low-engagement posts.

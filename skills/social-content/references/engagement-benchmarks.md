# Engagement Benchmarks Reference

## Engagement Rate Benchmarks by Platform

Engagement rate = (total engagements / impressions) x 100. These are median rates; top performers hit 2-3x these numbers.

### LinkedIn
| Account Size | Good ER | Great ER | Exceptional ER |
|---|---|---|---|
| Under 5K followers | 3-5% | 5-8% | 8%+ |
| 5K-25K followers | 2-4% | 4-6% | 6%+ |
| 25K-100K followers | 1.5-3% | 3-5% | 5%+ |
| 100K+ followers | 1-2% | 2-3% | 3%+ |

**Key insight:** LinkedIn engagement rates decrease with follower count because larger audiences include more passive followers. A 5% ER at 10K followers is more impressive than 2% at 100K.

### Twitter/X
| Account Size | Good ER | Great ER | Exceptional ER |
|---|---|---|---|
| Under 5K followers | 1-3% | 3-5% | 5%+ |
| 5K-25K followers | 0.5-2% | 2-3% | 3%+ |
| 25K-100K followers | 0.3-1% | 1-2% | 2%+ |
| 100K+ followers | 0.1-0.5% | 0.5-1% | 1%+ |

**Key insight:** Twitter ER is naturally lower because of high posting volume and fast-moving feeds. Judge threads separately from single tweets -- a thread with 1% ER on tweet 1 can have 5%+ ER on the full thread view.

### Instagram
| Account Size | Good ER | Great ER | Exceptional ER |
|---|---|---|---|
| Under 5K followers | 4-6% | 6-10% | 10%+ |
| 5K-25K followers | 2-4% | 4-6% | 6%+ |
| 25K-100K followers | 1.5-3% | 3-5% | 5%+ |
| 100K+ followers | 1-2% | 2-3% | 3%+ |

**Key insight:** Instagram save rate is more important than engagement rate for algorithm distribution. Benchmark: If 2%+ of viewers save your post, you're creating high-value content the algorithm will push.

### TikTok
| Account Size | Good ER | Great ER | Exceptional ER |
|---|---|---|---|
| Under 10K followers | 5-8% | 8-15% | 15%+ |
| 10K-100K followers | 3-6% | 6-10% | 10%+ |
| 100K-1M followers | 2-4% | 4-8% | 8%+ |
| 1M+ followers | 1-3% | 3-5% | 5%+ |

**Key insight:** TikTok's algorithm is the most follower-agnostic. A new account can get millions of views on a single video. Completion rate matters more than ER -- if 50%+ of viewers watch to the end, the algorithm pushes it to the next audience tier.

### Facebook
| Account Type | Good ER | Great ER | Exceptional ER |
|---|---|---|---|
| Page (organic) | 0.5-1% | 1-2% | 2%+ |
| Group post | 2-5% | 5-10% | 10%+ |
| Personal profile | 1-3% | 3-5% | 5%+ |

**Key insight:** Facebook Pages have severely depressed organic reach (1-5% of followers). Groups are the only high-engagement channel left on Facebook. If community is your goal, run a Group, not a Page.

## Algorithm Ranking Signals by Platform

### LinkedIn Algorithm (2024-2025 model)
1. **Dwell time** (how long people read your post) -- strongest signal
2. **Meaningful comments** (10+ word comments weighted 3x vs "Great post!")
3. **Shares via DM** (private shares signal high-value content)
4. **Engagement velocity** (first 60 minutes determines distribution tier)
5. **Reactions** (least weighted, but "Insightful" > "Like")
6. **Creator credibility** (consistent posting history, profile completeness)

**Algorithm penalty triggers:**
- External links in post body (-40-50% reach)
- Editing post within first 10 minutes (-distribution restart)
- Engagement pods (LinkedIn detects coordinated engagement)
- Posting more than once per 18-24 hours (second post cannibalizes first)

### Twitter/X Algorithm
1. **Reply depth** (conversations = extended distribution)
2. **Quote tweets with commentary** (signals content worth discussing)
3. **Retweets** (direct distribution to new audiences)
4. **Engagement velocity** (first 30 minutes critical)
5. **Bookmarks** (save signal = high-value content)
6. **Profile visits from tweet** (signals curiosity)

**Algorithm boosts:**
- Premium/Blue subscribers get ~2x distribution weight
- Threads keep users on platform longer (rewarded)
- Images/video tweets get 1.5-2x reach vs text-only

### Instagram Algorithm
1. **Saves** (highest-weighted signal -- indicates reference-worthy content)
2. **Shares via DM** (indicates "this person needs to see this")
3. **Comments** (especially longer comments)
4. **Watch time** (for Reels: completion rate is the #1 signal)
5. **Follows from content** (signals discovery-worthy content)
6. **Likes** (lowest weight, but high volume still matters)

**Algorithm notes:**
- Reels shown to non-followers 3-5x more than feed posts
- Carousels shown to followers repeatedly until they engage (swipe = engagement)
- Instagram deprioritizes accounts that post content from other apps (watermarked TikToks)

### TikTok Algorithm
1. **Completion rate** (% who watch the full video -- THE dominant signal)
2. **Rewatch rate** (people watching 2+ times)
3. **Shares** (strongest distribution multiplier)
4. **Comments** (volume and sentiment)
5. **Follows from video** (signals account worth discovering)
6. **Profile visits** (curiosity signal)

**TikTok distribution tiers:**
- Tier 1: ~200-500 views (initial test audience)
- Tier 2: ~1K-5K views (passed initial engagement threshold)
- Tier 3: ~10K-50K views (strong engagement signals)
- Tier 4: ~100K+ views (viral trajectory)
- Video must pass each tier's engagement threshold to advance

## Content Type Performance Benchmarks

### Format Performance Ranking by Platform

**LinkedIn:**
1. Document/carousel posts (2.5x average reach)
2. Text-only posts with line breaks (1.5x)
3. Native video (1.3x)
4. Image posts (1.0x baseline)
5. Link posts (0.5x -- penalized by algorithm)

**Twitter/X:**
1. Threads with images (2.0x average engagement)
2. Tweet with image/video (1.5x)
3. Quote tweets with insight (1.3x)
4. Text-only tweets (1.0x baseline)
5. Link tweets (0.7x)

**Instagram:**
1. Reels (3-5x reach vs feed posts for non-followers)
2. Carousels (1.5-2x engagement vs single image)
3. Single image (1.0x baseline)
4. Stories (reach = 5-15% of followers, but high engagement from those who view)

**TikTok:**
1. Trending sound + original content (highest For You Page chance)
2. Original sound with text overlay (standard performance)
3. Duets/Stitches (variable -- depends on original video's audience)
4. Photo slideshow mode (lower engagement than video)

## Viral Content Pattern Data

Content that breaks out of normal distribution shares specific characteristics.

### Viral Triggers (ranked by frequency in top 0.1% posts)
1. **Specificity + surprise**: A specific number or result that defies expectations ("I made $0 from 100K followers. Here's what happened.")
2. **Contrarian + evidence**: An opinion that opposes mainstream thinking, backed by data or experience
3. **Vulnerability + lesson**: Personal failure or struggle with a transferable takeaway
4. **Utility + urgency**: Immediately actionable information that feels time-sensitive
5. **Identity reinforcement**: Content that makes a specific group feel seen ("If you're a founder who [specific experience], you know this feeling.")

### What Does NOT Cause Virality (Common Myths)
- Posting at the "perfect" time (matters <5% vs content quality)
- Using specific hashtags (helps discovery, doesn't cause virality)
- Polished production quality (raw/authentic often outperforms polished)
- Posting frequency alone (10 bad posts don't equal 1 good one)
- Asking people to "like and share" (explicit engagement begging is penalized on most platforms)

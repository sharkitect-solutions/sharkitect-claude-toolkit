# Copy Diagnostics: Identifying and Fixing Weak Copy

## The Copy Diagnostic Framework

Use this framework to audit existing copy OR to self-check before delivering a draft.

### 5-Point Diagnostic Scan

Run these checks in order. Each catches a different class of copy failure.

#### 1. Value Proposition Check
**Question:** Can a stranger read only the headline and subheadline and tell you:
- WHO this is for
- WHAT they get
- WHY it matters

**If no:** The above-fold copy is failing its primary job.

**Before (fails):**
> "Innovating the Future of Work"
> "Our platform leverages cutting-edge AI to streamline your operations."

**After (passes):**
> "Cut Your Weekly Reporting from 4 Hours to 15 Minutes"
> "Automated dashboards pull data from 50+ tools so your team ships insights, not spreadsheets."

**Why the before fails:** "Innovating" and "leverages" are empty abstractions. No one wakes up wanting "streamlined operations." The after gives a specific, measurable outcome.

---

#### 2. Specificity Audit
**Question:** For every claim, is there a number, name, timeframe, or concrete detail?

**Before (vague):**
> "Save time with our fast onboarding process."

**After (specific):**
> "Most teams are running their first campaign within 23 minutes -- no training required."

**Before (vague):**
> "Trusted by thousands of companies worldwide."

**After (specific):**
> "Used by 12,400 teams across 47 countries, including Stripe, Notion, and Linear."

**Rule:** If you can swap the product name and the claim still works for a competitor, the copy is too vague.

---

#### 3. "So What?" Cascade
**Question:** Does every claim answer the reader's silent "so what?"

Read each sentence and ask "so what?" If the copy doesn't answer it, add the answer.

**Before:**
> "AI-powered analytics"

So what? -- "See which pages lose customers before they buy"

So what? -- "Fix the leaks and recover up to 15% of lost revenue"

**The final "so what?" answer is usually the real headline.**

---

#### 4. Voice Match Check
**Question:** Does this sound like how the customer talks, or how the company talks?

**Company voice (bad):**
> "Our solution facilitates seamless cross-functional collaboration through an integrated workspace."

**Customer voice (good):**
> "Finally, one place where marketing and sales can actually see what each other is doing."

**How to fix:** Pull real phrases from customer reviews, support tickets, and sales calls. Use their exact words.

---

#### 5. Action Clarity Check
**Question:** At any point on the page, does the reader know exactly what to do next?

**Fails when:**
- Multiple CTAs compete for attention (3+ CTAs visible at once)
- CTA text is vague ("Learn More", "Get Started")
- No CTA is visible without scrolling on mobile
- The CTA asks for too much too soon (pricing page CTA on a problem-unaware visitor)

**Fix:** One primary CTA per viewport. CTA text states what they GET, not what they DO.

## Before/After Transformation Library

### Transformation 1: Feature-First to Benefit-First

**Before:**
> **Real-time Collaboration**
> Edit documents simultaneously with your team. See changes as they happen with our conflict-free editing engine.

**After:**
> **Stop Emailing Drafts Back and Forth**
> Your whole team edits the same document at once -- no more "final_v3_ACTUAL_final.doc." Every change appears instantly, and nothing ever gets overwritten.

**What changed:** Led with the pain (emailing drafts), named a specific symptom (version naming chaos), THEN explained the mechanism.

---

### Transformation 2: Generic Testimonial to Specific Proof

**Before:**
> "Great product! Really helped our team." -- John S.

**After:**
> "We went from 6-hour weekly reports to a 15-minute automated dashboard. I got my Fridays back." -- John Sullivan, VP Marketing, Acme Corp

**What changed:** Added a specific result, full attribution (name, role, company), and a human detail ("got my Fridays back").

---

### Transformation 3: Corporate Jargon to Human Copy

**Before:**
> Leverage our comprehensive suite of integrated solutions to optimize your operational efficiency and drive sustainable growth across your organization.

**After:**
> Run your business with fewer tools, less busywork, and more time for the work that actually matters.

**What changed:** Removed every abstraction. Replaced corporate language with plain English. Said the same thing in 60% fewer words.

---

### Transformation 4: Weak CTA to Strong CTA

**Before:**
> [Learn More] [Sign Up] [Contact Us]

**After:**
> [See Acme in Action -- Watch 2-Min Demo]
> Secondary: [Start My Free Trial -- No Credit Card]

**What changed:** Collapsed 3 competing CTAs to 1 primary + 1 secondary. Added specificity (2-min demo, no credit card). First-person "My" increases ownership.

---

### Transformation 5: Wall of Text to Scannable Copy

**Before:**
> Our platform provides a comprehensive set of tools for managing your team's projects. With features like task management, time tracking, file sharing, and communication tools, you can keep everything in one place. Teams that use our platform report increased productivity and better collaboration across departments.

**After:**
> **Everything your team needs. Nothing it doesn't.**
>
> - **Tasks:** Assign, track, and close -- no switching tabs
> - **Time:** See where hours go without manual timesheets
> - **Files:** Share and find anything in 2 clicks
> - **Chat:** Talk where the work happens
>
> Teams ship 30% faster in their first month. [See how ->]

**What changed:** Broke the wall into a scannable list. Added a concrete proof point. Each bullet leads with the benefit, not the feature name.

---

### Transformation 6: Same Copy for All Traffic vs Source-Matched

**Before (used for ALL traffic):**
> "The Best Project Management Tool for Teams"
> Sign up and start managing your projects today.

**After (tailored to traffic source):**

From Google search "project management for agencies":
> "Project Management Built for Agencies"
> "Track client work, billable hours, and team capacity in one dashboard."

From competitor comparison ad:
> "Switching from Monday.com? Here is What You Get."
> "Same features, half the price, and unlimited users on every plan."

From retargeting ad:
> "Still Thinking It Over?"
> "12,000 teams made the switch this quarter. Start your free trial -- takes 60 seconds."

**What changed:** Each version matches the visitor's mental state on arrival instead of using one-size-fits-all messaging.

## Copy Scoring Rubric

Use this to grade copy quality on a quick 1-5 scale per dimension.

| Dimension | 1 (Weak) | 3 (Average) | 5 (Expert) |
|-----------|----------|-------------|------------|
| Specificity | Buzzwords, no numbers | Some detail, occasional proof | Every claim has data, names, or concrete examples |
| Voice match | Sounds like a press release | Professional but generic | Sounds exactly like the customer talks |
| Benefit clarity | Feature list only | Benefits mentioned but vague | Every feature tied to a specific outcome |
| Proof density | Zero evidence | One testimonial or stat | Proof integrated throughout (social, data, stories) |
| Scannability | Wall of text | Some headers and breaks | Clear hierarchy, scannable sections, visual variety |
| CTA strength | "Submit" / "Learn More" | Decent but generic | Specific, first-person, value-driven, one primary |
| Mobile readability | Requires horizontal scroll | Readable but cramped | Designed for thumb-scrolling, short paragraphs |
| Emotional resonance | Flat corporate tone | Some personality | Reader feels "this was written for me" |

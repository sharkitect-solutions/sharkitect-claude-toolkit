# Qualification Frameworks -- Detailed Reference

## BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)

**Best for:** SMB deals < $50K, single decision maker, known budget categories.

| Dimension | Research Question | Where to Find Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Budget | Does this company typically spend in this category? What's their approximate range? | Funding data, employee count (proxy for revenue), job postings mentioning tools/budgets, industry benchmarks |
| Authority | Who owns the buying decision for this category? | Org chart (LinkedIn), job descriptions referencing "vendor selection" or "tool evaluation" |
| Need | Is there an active pain point your product addresses? | Job postings for roles your product augments/replaces, reviews of competitors, community complaints, recent operational changes |
| Timeline | Is there urgency or a forcing function? | Contract renewal cycles (typical by industry), fiscal year timing, regulatory deadlines, leadership changes creating mandate |

**BANT scoring weights:** Need (35%) > Authority (25%) > Timeline (25%) > Budget (15%). Budget is last because it's the most flexible dimension -- companies find budget for urgent needs.

**Common BANT mistake:** Treating BANT as a checklist (all four must be "yes"). In practice, strong Need + clear Authority can overcome Budget and Timeline uncertainty. Score accordingly.

## MEDDIC (Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion)

**Best for:** Enterprise deals > $50K, committee buying, long sales cycles (3-12 months).

| Dimension | Research Question | Where to Find Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Metrics | What measurable outcomes does the prospect care about? What KPIs would your product impact? | Annual reports, earnings calls, job descriptions mentioning metrics, industry benchmark reports |
| Economic Buyer | Who signs the check? (Not who evaluates -- who approves spend.) | LinkedIn seniority mapping, org chart, procurement team existence (suggests formal process) |
| Decision Criteria | What factors will they use to compare options? | RFP patterns in their industry, analyst reports they consume, competitor evaluations they've done (G2 reviews authored BY them) |
| Decision Process | How many steps and stakeholders between interest and purchase? | Company size (proxy), procurement department existence, compliance requirements in their industry |
| Identify Pain | What specific, quantifiable pain exists RIGHT NOW? | Job postings, tech stack gaps, customer complaints about their product (they feel downstream pain), operational bottlenecks visible in hiring patterns |
| Champion | Is there someone inside who would advocate for your solution? | Conference speakers on relevant topics, content authors, LinkedIn posts about the problem, previous company alumni who used your product |

**MEDDIC scoring approach:** Champion and Identify Pain are the two highest-weight dimensions. Without both, enterprise deals stall regardless of other factors. If you can't identify a plausible champion path, score the lead 2 points lower.

## CHAMP (Challenges, Authority, Money, Prioritization)

**Best for:** Mid-market deals where the pain is visible and the buyer needs to see solution fit. Products that solve known, painful problems rather than creating new categories.

| Dimension | Research Question | Where to Find Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Challenges | What specific operational challenges does this company face that your product addresses? | Support forums, Glassdoor reviews (employee complaints about processes), social media from employees, job descriptions mentioning "improving" or "fixing" specific processes |
| Authority | Who is empowered to solve this challenge? | Org chart, recently created roles (new VP of X = mandate to fix X), job postings for the relevant department |
| Money | Can they afford your solution? Is budget allocated for this problem? | Revenue proxies, funding, existing spend in adjacent categories, job postings mentioning tool budgets |
| Prioritization | Is solving this challenge a current priority or a someday-maybe? | Board-level mentions (public companies), OKR signals in job postings, urgency language in job descriptions, recent negative events that elevate priority |

**CHAMP advantage over BANT:** Starts with the customer's world (challenges) rather than your sales process (budget). Produces more empathetic outreach angles.

## GPCTBA&CI (Goals, Plans, Challenges, Timeline, Budget, Authority, Consequences, Implications)

**Best for:** Consultative sales where the buyer hasn't fully articulated the problem. Complex products that require education. New category creation.

This is the deepest framework and the most research-intensive. Only use when deal size justifies the investment (typically > $30K ACV or strategic accounts).

| Dimension | Research Question |
|---|---|
| Goals | What are the company's stated strategic goals for this year/quarter? |
| Plans | What initiatives have they announced to achieve those goals? |
| Challenges | What obstacles stand between their current state and their goals? |
| Timeline | When do they need to achieve these goals? What's the forcing function? |
| Budget | What resources have they allocated to these initiatives? |
| Authority | Who owns the goal and the budget for achieving it? |
| Consequences | What happens if they DON'T solve this? (Quantify the cost of inaction.) |
| Implications | What positive outcomes emerge if they DO solve this? (Quantify the ROI.) |

**GPCTBA&CI research strategy:** Start with Goals (public: annual reports, press releases, leadership interviews). Work backwards to Challenges (what's preventing goal achievement). The Consequences/Implications pair is your outreach hook -- "If you don't solve X, Y happens; if you do, Z is possible."

## Framework Comparison Matrix

| Factor | BANT | MEDDIC | CHAMP | GPCTBA&CI |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Research time per lead | 15-30 min | 45-90 min | 20-40 min | 60-120 min |
| Best deal size | < $50K | > $50K | $10K-$75K | > $30K |
| Stakeholder complexity | 1-2 | 3-7 | 2-4 | 2-5 |
| Requires champion identification | No | Yes (critical) | No | No |
| Pain must be known/visible | Helpful | Required | Required | Not required |
| Works for new categories | Poorly | Poorly | Poorly | Well |
| Outreach personalization depth | Low | High | Medium | Very High |

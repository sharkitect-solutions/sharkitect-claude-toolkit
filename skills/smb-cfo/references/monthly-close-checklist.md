# Monthly Close Checklist for SMBs

## Overview

Complete the monthly close within 5 business days of month-end. Speed matters because decisions made on stale data compound errors. The goal is accurate financials available by Day 6 for management review.

---

## Day 1: Revenue and Receivables

### Revenue Recognition
- [ ] Confirm all invoices for the month are issued (no unbilled revenue left behind)
- [ ] Apply revenue recognition rules: recognize only revenue where delivery obligation is met
- [ ] For subscription businesses: confirm MRR matches active subscriptions; reconcile any mid-month upgrades, downgrades, or cancellations
- [ ] Record deferred revenue for payments received but not yet earned (annual prepayments, milestone billing)
- [ ] Reverse any prior-month deferrals that are now earned

### Accounts Receivable
- [ ] Generate AR aging report (Current, 1-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+ days)
- [ ] Flag all invoices over 60 days -- assign to collections workflow
- [ ] Record bad debt allowance: estimate uncollectible percentage based on historical write-off rate (typical SMB: 1-3% of total AR)
- [ ] Reconcile AR subledger to general ledger total -- discrepancies indicate missed invoices or unapplied payments
- [ ] Document any customer disputes or credit memos issued during the month

---

## Day 2: Expenses and Payables

### Expense Reconciliation
- [ ] Match all bank transactions to expense categories -- zero unclassified transactions
- [ ] Review corporate credit card statements; obtain receipts for any charge over $75
- [ ] Accrue known expenses where invoices have not yet arrived (utility estimates, contractor hours submitted but not invoiced)
- [ ] Verify prepaid expense amortization (insurance, annual software) is recorded for the month
- [ ] Capitalize any qualifying expenditure (>$2,500 and >1 year useful life) -- do not expense

### Accounts Payable
- [ ] Generate AP aging report; confirm no invoices are past due without authorization
- [ ] Match purchase orders to invoices to receiving records (three-way match for physical goods)
- [ ] Accrue any received-not-invoiced items
- [ ] Verify payment terms compliance -- early payment discounts captured, late fees avoided
- [ ] Reconcile AP subledger to general ledger

---

## Day 3: Payroll and Tax Accruals

### Payroll Reconciliation
- [ ] Confirm gross payroll matches headcount report (no phantom employees, no missed hires)
- [ ] Reconcile employer-side taxes: FICA, FUTA, SUTA match expected rates
- [ ] Verify benefits deductions align with enrollment records
- [ ] Accrue any bonus, commission, or PTO liability earned but not yet paid
- [ ] For contractor payments: confirm 1099-eligible payments are tracked and classified separately from W-2 payroll

### Tax Liability Accruals
- [ ] Calculate monthly income tax accrual based on year-to-date effective rate
- [ ] Accrue sales tax collected but not yet remitted -- reconcile to sales tax returns
- [ ] Accrue payroll tax liabilities due in the following month
- [ ] Verify quarterly estimated tax payments are on schedule (federal: Apr/Jun/Sep/Jan)
- [ ] Record any tax credits earned during the month (R&D credit, WOTC, state incentives)

---

## Day 4: Bank Reconciliation and Adjustments

### Bank Reconciliation
- [ ] Reconcile every bank account: GL ending balance must match bank statement adjusted for outstanding items
- [ ] Identify all outstanding checks older than 90 days -- investigate and void if stale
- [ ] Match all electronic transfers, ACH debits, and wire transfers to GL entries
- [ ] Record bank fees and interest income/expense
- [ ] Investigate and resolve any unreconciled differences above $100

### Journal Entry Review
- [ ] Review all manual journal entries posted during the month -- each must have supporting documentation
- [ ] Verify intercompany entries net to zero (if applicable)
- [ ] Confirm recurring journal entries posted correctly (depreciation, amortization, rent straight-lining)
- [ ] Review and adjust any suspense account balances -- clear all items or document reason for carry-forward
- [ ] Ensure no entries bypass approval workflow

---

## Day 5: Reporting and Management Package

### Financial Statement Preparation
- [ ] Generate P&L: actual vs budget vs prior month vs prior year same month
- [ ] Generate balance sheet: compare to prior month, investigate material changes (>5%)
- [ ] Generate cash flow statement (indirect method for monthly, direct method for 13-week forecast update)
- [ ] Calculate key metrics: gross margin, operating margin, net margin, runway, burn rate

### Management Reporting Package
Include these components for executive/board review:
1. **P&L summary** with variance commentary on items deviating >10% from budget
2. **Cash position** and updated runway calculation
3. **AR aging summary** with collection forecast
4. **Headcount report** showing actual vs planned
5. **Key metrics dashboard**: MRR/ARR (SaaS), revenue per employee, LTV:CAC, burn rate
6. **Forward-looking items**: known large expenses, expected contract signings, cash flow risks

### Close Certification
- [ ] CFO/controller reviews and signs off on all reconciliations
- [ ] Lock the accounting period to prevent backdated entries
- [ ] File close documentation (reconciliations, JE support, variance explanations) in month folder
- [ ] Note any items requiring follow-up in next month's open items list

---

## Common Close Errors

| Error | Impact | Prevention |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Booking revenue before delivery | Overstates current-month revenue and margin | Apply rev rec checklist on Day 1; no invoice without delivery confirmation |
| Missing expense accruals | Understates expenses, inflates profit | Maintain recurring accrual template; review prior month accruals as starting checklist |
| Unreconciled bank balance | Hides errors, potential fraud | Zero tolerance for unreconciled amounts; investigate every difference on Day 4 |
| Miscoded expense categories | Distorts department budgets and tax deductions | Use chart of accounts with descriptions; review uncategorized transactions daily |
| Stale outstanding checks | Overstates cash committed, understates available | Void checks outstanding >90 days; reissue only if vendor confirms non-receipt |
| Payroll accrual gaps | Understates largest expense category | Reconcile headcount to payroll register every month; accrue unpaid periods |

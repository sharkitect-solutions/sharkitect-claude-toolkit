# Email Sequence Timing & Benchmark Data

## Open Rate by Day of Week

| Day | B2B Open Rate | B2C Open Rate | Notes |
|-----|--------------|--------------|-------|
| Monday | 18-22% | 19-23% | Inbox competition from weekend backlog |
| Tuesday | 22-28% | 20-25% | Best performing B2B day consistently |
| Wednesday | 21-27% | 20-25% | Second-best B2B day |
| Thursday | 20-26% | 21-26% | Good all-around |
| Friday | 16-21% | 18-23% | B2B drops off hard after 2pm |
| Saturday | 10-15% | 22-28% | B2B dead zone, B2C can outperform weekdays |
| Sunday | 11-16% | 21-27% | Same pattern — B2C leisure browsing peaks |

**Send time sweet spots:**
- B2B: 9:00-11:00 AM recipient local time (peak), 1:00-3:00 PM (secondary)
- B2C: 7:00-9:00 AM (productivity/health), 7:00-9:00 PM (lifestyle/entertainment)
- Universal: Avoid 12:00-1:00 PM (lunch scroll = opens without engagement)

Normal decay pattern — each email loses a percentage of the previous email's opens:

| Email Position | Onboarding | Nurture | Re-engagement | Win-back |
|---------------|-----------|---------|---------------|----------|
| Email #1 | 60-75% | 45-60% | 25-35% | 20-30% |
| Email #2 | 50-65% | 35-50% | 18-28% | 15-22% |
| Email #3 | 42-55% | 28-42% | 12-22% | 10-18% |
| Email #4 | 35-48% | 24-36% | 10-18% | — |
| Email #5 | 30-42% | 20-32% | — | — |
| Email #6 | 28-38% | 18-28% | — | — |
| Email #7 | 25-35% | 16-25% | — | — |
| Email #8 | — | 14-22% | — | — |

**Normal decay rate:** 8-15% drop per email
**Concerning decay rate:** 20%+ drop between any two consecutive emails
**Healthy sign:** Open rate INCREASES on an email (means previous email created strong anticipation)

## Unsubscribe Rate Thresholds

| Rate per Email | Assessment | Action |
|---------------|------------|--------|
| <0.2% | Healthy | Continue as-is |
| 0.2-0.5% | Normal | Monitor, no alarm |
| 0.5-1.0% | Elevated | Review email content and frequency |
| 1.0-2.0% | Problematic | Specific email needs revision — check tone, relevance, frequency |
| >2.0% | Critical | Stop sequence. Investigate. Likely deliverability or relevance failure. |

**Key insight:** Unsubscribe spikes on a SPECIFIC email (not gradual increase) almost always mean that email is off-tone, overly aggressive, or irrelevant to the audience's expectations set by previous emails.

## Subject Line Performance Data

### By Length
| Character Count | Avg Open Rate Impact | Notes |
|----------------|---------------------|-------|
| <20 chars | -5 to -10% | Too vague, looks auto-generated |
| 20-35 chars | Baseline | Works on mobile, clear |
| 35-50 chars | +5 to +12% | Sweet spot — enough info for decision |
| 50-70 chars | Baseline | Desktop fine, mobile truncation risk |
| >70 chars | -8 to -15% | Truncated everywhere, looks spammy |

### By Pattern
| Pattern | Avg Open Rate | Best For |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Question ("Still struggling with X?") | 28-35% | Mid-sequence re-engagement |
| How-to ("How to [outcome] in [time]") | 25-32% | Teaching emails |
| Number ("3 ways to...") | 24-30% | Early sequence, curiosity |
| Direct address ("[Name], your...") | 26-34% | Late sequence (email 4+) |
| Story tease ("The mistake I made...") | 27-33% | Mid-sequence, stories |
| Urgency ("Last chance:...") | 22-38% | Final emails ONLY (loses power if overused) |
| Curiosity gap ("This changed everything") | 20-35% | High variance — depends on trust level |

### By Emoji Usage
| Usage | Impact | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| No emoji | Baseline | Safe, professional |
| 1 relevant emoji at start | +2 to +5% | Catches eye in inbox, B2C |
| 1 relevant emoji mid-line | +1 to +3% | Subtle, less polarizing |
| Multiple emojis | -5 to -15% | Looks spammy, especially B2B |
| Emoji-only subject | -20%+ | Spam filter trigger, avoid always |

## Optimal Sequence Length by Product Type

| Product Type | Price Point | Recommended Emails | Recommended Duration |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| SaaS free trial | $0 → $X/mo | 5-7 | 14 days |
| SaaS freemium upgrade | Free → paid | 3-5 | 7-14 days (trigger-based) |
| Online course | $50-$500 | 5-8 | 10-14 days |
| High-ticket service | $1K-$10K+ | 8-12 | 21-45 days |
| E-commerce (first purchase) | $20-$200 | 3-5 | 5-10 days |
| B2B enterprise | $10K+ | 10-15 | 30-90 days |
| Info product / ebook | <$50 | 3-5 | 5-7 days |
| Membership / community | $10-$100/mo | 4-6 | 10-14 days |

**The rule:** Higher price = longer sequence = more trust-building required. A $29 ebook doesn't need a 12-email nurture. A $15K consulting engagement does.

## Email #1 Timing Impact Data

| Delivery Speed | Open Rate Impact | Conversion Impact |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| <5 minutes | Baseline (highest) | Baseline (highest) |
| 5-30 minutes | -5 to -10% | -8 to -12% |
| 30-60 minutes | -10 to -20% | -15 to -25% |
| 1-4 hours | -20 to -35% | -25 to -40% |
| 4-24 hours | -35 to -50% | -40 to -60% |
| >24 hours | -50%+ | -60%+ |

**This is the single highest-impact timing decision in any sequence.** A perfectly written email #1 sent 4 hours late will underperform a mediocre email #1 sent in 2 minutes. Automate trigger-based sends. Never batch.

# Content Distribution Reference

Expert decision layer for distributing content across channels. Platform-specific social media tactics live in the social-content skill. This reference covers the DISTRIBUTION STRATEGY that content-creator needs: where to push content, when, and how channels work together.

## Distribution Channel Selection by Content Type

Not all content belongs everywhere. Match the content type to the channels where it earns attention.

| Content Type | Primary Channel | Secondary Channels | Channels to Skip |
|-------------|----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Blog post (educational) | Organic search (SEO) | LinkedIn, email newsletter, Twitter thread | Instagram (unless visual), TikTok |
| Blog post (opinion/thought leadership) | LinkedIn | Twitter, email, industry communities | Search (opinion pieces rarely rank) |
| Case study | Email nurture sequences, sales enablement | LinkedIn, website resource hub | Social (too long for feeds -- excerpt only) |
| Whitepaper/report | Gated landing page + email | LinkedIn (executive summary), PR outreach | Social feeds (use teaser stats only) |
| Email newsletter | Email (primary by definition) | Repurpose best items to LinkedIn/blog | Do not cross-post full newsletters to social |
| Infographic | Pinterest, LinkedIn | Blog embed, Twitter (key stat excerpts) | Email (renders poorly on mobile) |
| Video (long-form) | YouTube | Blog embed, LinkedIn, email | Twitter (short attention span) |
| Video (short-form) | TikTok, Instagram Reels | YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn | Email, blog (embed only if supporting text) |

## Paid vs. Organic Distribution Decisions

```
Should you pay to distribute this piece?
|
+-- Is the content gated (lead gen)?
|   +-- YES --> Paid distribution justified. Target: lookalike audiences of existing customers.
|   +-- NO --> Organic first. Only boost if organic traction proves the content resonates.
|
+-- Is there a time-sensitive element (event, launch, seasonal)?
|   +-- YES --> Paid to accelerate. Organic alone is too slow for deadlines.
|   +-- NO --> Let organic run 48-72 hours first. Data from organic reach informs paid targeting.
|
+-- Is this a cornerstone/pillar piece you want to own long-term?
|   +-- YES --> Invest in SEO (organic) over paid. Paid traffic stops when budget stops.
|   +-- NO --> Paid boost for short-term visibility is fine.
```

**Budget allocation rule of thumb:** For most content marketing programs, 70% organic / 30% paid. Shift to 50/50 only for product launches or time-sensitive campaigns. Never go 100% paid -- it masks whether the content is actually good.

## Content Lifecycle Management

Every piece of content has a lifecycle. Manage it or watch traffic decay.

**Refresh triggers (update and republish):**
- Traffic has declined 30%+ from peak (check quarterly)
- Core data or statistics are more than 18 months old
- Search intent has shifted (check "People Also Ask" for new questions)
- A competitor published a better version of the same topic

**Refresh process:**
1. Update outdated stats, examples, and screenshots
2. Add any new sections that address gaps (check search console for queries the page ranks for but doesn't explicitly answer)
3. Improve the hook if the original was weak
4. Keep the same URL. Republish with updated date.
5. Re-promote through email and social as if it were new content

**Retire triggers (redirect or archive):**
- Topic is no longer relevant to your business
- Content has zero search impressions for 6+ months
- Better content on your own site covers the same topic (consolidate and 301 redirect)

**Content age by type:**
- Evergreen guides: 18-24 month shelf life before needing refresh
- Data-driven content: 6-12 months (data ages fast)
- Opinion/thought leadership: 12-18 months (perspectives evolve)
- News/trend pieces: 1-3 months (then archive or redirect)

## Cross-Channel Coordination

Blog, email, and social are not three separate programs. They are one distribution system with three surfaces.

**The coordination sequence for a major content piece:**

| Day | Channel | Action |
|-----|---------|--------|
| Day 0 (publish) | Blog/website | Publish the pillar piece |
| Day 0 | Social (LinkedIn + Twitter) | First-wave promotion: contrarian hook + link |
| Day 1 | Email | Newsletter feature or dedicated send to relevant segment |
| Day 2-3 | Social (Instagram, other) | Derivative content: carousel, quote graphic, short video |
| Week 1-2 | Social | Drip remaining derivatives (2-3 per week) |
| Week 2 | Blog | Internal link from related existing posts to new piece |
| Week 3-4 | Email | Reference in future newsletters if still relevant |
| Month 2-3 | Social | Reshare top-performing derivative with fresh framing |

**Coordination rules:**
- Email subscribers see it first or same-day. They opted in -- reward them.
- Social gets the angle, not the full content. The goal is to drive clicks, not replace the blog post.
- Never publish the same text across platforms. Each platform gets a native version.
- Track which channel drives the most engaged traffic (not just clicks -- look at time on page and conversions).

## Performance Benchmarks by Content Type

What "good" looks like, so you can evaluate whether distribution is working.

| Content Type | Key Metric | Baseline (acceptable) | Good | Excellent |
|-------------|-----------|----------------------|------|-----------|
| Blog post | Organic sessions/month (after 3 months) | 200-500 | 500-2,000 | 2,000+ |
| Blog post | Avg time on page | 1:30 | 2:30-4:00 | 4:00+ |
| Case study | Conversion rate (to next step) | 2-5% | 5-10% | 10%+ |
| Whitepaper | Download rate (from landing page) | 10-20% | 20-35% | 35%+ |
| Email newsletter | Open rate | 18-22% | 22-30% | 30%+ |
| Email newsletter | Click-through rate | 1.5-2.5% | 2.5-5% | 5%+ |
| LinkedIn post | Engagement rate | 1-2% | 2-5% | 5%+ |
| Twitter/X thread | Engagement rate | 0.5-1% | 1-3% | 3%+ |

**Benchmark context:** These ranges are industry-wide medians. B2B benchmarks trend lower on volume but higher on conversion. B2C trends higher on reach but lower on per-interaction value. Niche audiences (<10K total addressable) will have lower absolute numbers but often higher engagement rates.

**What to do when benchmarks are not met:**
- Below baseline for 2+ months: the content or the channel choice is wrong. Re-evaluate both.
- At baseline but not growing: distribution is working but content quality or angle needs improvement.
- Above good but declining: the content is aging. Refresh or replace.

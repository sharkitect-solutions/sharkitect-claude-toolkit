# QBR Playbook - Deep Reference

## QBR Cadence by Account Tier

| Tier | ACV | QBR Frequency | Duration | Attendees (Customer Side) | Attendees (Your Side) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Enterprise | >$100K | Quarterly | 60 min | Exec sponsor + 2-3 stakeholders | CSM + CS Director + Product liaison |
| Mid-Market | $25K-$100K | Semi-annual | 45 min | Primary contact + 1 stakeholder | CSM + optional CS Director |
| SMB | $5K-$25K | Annual | 30 min | Primary contact | CSM |
| Self-Serve | <$5K | None (tech-touch only) | -- | -- | -- |

Schedule QBRs 3+ weeks in advance. Executive calendars fill fast. Sending invites with less than 2 weeks notice signals the QBR is not important enough to plan for.

## Pre-QBR Preparation Checklist (Complete 5 Business Days Before)

### Data Pull
- [ ] Current health score with all five dimension breakdowns
- [ ] Usage analytics: DAU/MAU trend, feature adoption changes, top features used
- [ ] Support metrics: tickets opened/resolved, CSAT trend, open escalations
- [ ] Financial summary: contract value, renewal date, expansion history, outstanding invoices
- [ ] NPS/CSAT scores: most recent survey responses with verbatim comments

### Stakeholder Research
- [ ] Confirm exec sponsor is still in role (check LinkedIn for job changes)
- [ ] Identify any new stakeholders since last QBR
- [ ] Review customer's recent company news (funding, reorgs, product launches, earnings)
- [ ] Check for any public competitor adoption signals
- [ ] Review internal notes from last QBR (action items, commitments made)

### Agenda Draft
- [ ] Send proposed agenda to customer 3 business days before QBR
- [ ] Ask customer to add their own agenda items
- [ ] Confirm attendee list on both sides
- [ ] Pre-seed one discussion question to encourage customer preparation

## QBR Agenda Template

### Opening (5 min)
State the purpose: align on goals, review value delivered, plan next quarter. Confirm agenda. Ask if the customer has items to add. Do not skip this -- it signals the meeting is collaborative, not a presentation.

### Customer Goals Review (10 min)
Review the goals established in the last QBR or success plan. Ask the customer to assess progress from their perspective first. Only then share your data. This sequence matters -- if you present first, customers default to agreeing rather than sharing candid assessment. Why: candid assessment reveals risks that data alone cannot surface.

### Value Delivered (15 min)
Present quantified ROI using the customer's own success metrics, not your product metrics. Structure as:
- **Investment**: What they paid (contract value + implementation effort)
- **Return**: Measurable outcomes tied to their stated goals (time saved, revenue generated, costs reduced, risk mitigated)
- **ROI calculation**: Return / Investment, annualized

If ROI cannot be quantified, present leading indicators: adoption rates, workflow efficiency gains, user satisfaction scores. Never present feature usage stats as value. "Your team used Feature X 500 times" is not value -- "Feature X saved your team 120 hours this quarter" is value.

### Roadmap Alignment (10 min)
Share 2-3 upcoming product capabilities relevant to this customer's goals. Frame each capability as: "You told us [goal]. We are building [feature] that addresses this by [mechanism]. Expected availability: [timeline]." Ask: "Does this align with where your priorities are heading?" Listen for shifted priorities that may require success plan updates.

### Open Discussion (10 min)
Reserve this time for the customer to raise anything not on the agenda. Prompt with: "What is the biggest challenge your team faces right now that we have not discussed?" This is where the real intelligence surfaces. Resist the urge to fill silence -- let the customer think and respond.

### Success Plan: Next Quarter (10 min)
Co-create 2-3 measurable objectives for the next period. Each objective follows the format:
- **Objective**: What the customer wants to achieve
- **Metric**: How success will be measured
- **Target**: Specific number or outcome
- **Your role**: What your team will do to support this
- **Customer action**: What the customer commits to

Both sides must have action items. A success plan with only vendor actions is a service plan, not a partnership.

## Post-QBR Process

### Within 24 Hours
- Send summary email to all attendees with: key discussion points, agreed objectives, action items with owners and deadlines, next QBR date
- Log QBR notes in CRM/CS platform with health score assessment
- Update customer success plan with new objectives

### Within 1 Week
- Begin executing on your action items
- Share any promised materials (reports, documentation, introductions)
- Schedule mid-quarter check-in if new risks or concerns surfaced

### Mid-Quarter
- Send progress update on action items
- Check in on customer's progress toward their committed actions
- Adjust success plan if circumstances changed

## QBR Anti-Patterns

### The Feature Demo Trap
Spending 30+ minutes showing new features. QBRs are not product marketing events. Features belong in release notes. QBRs are for strategic alignment. If the customer asks for a demo during a QBR, schedule a separate session: "Great interest -- let us set up a dedicated demo with your team next week so we can go deep."

### The One-Way Presentation
CSM presents slides for 45 minutes, asks "any questions?" with 3 minutes left. This is a lecture, not a business review. The 40% rule: the customer should speak at least 40% of the meeting time. If they speak less, the QBR failed regardless of content quality.

### The Missing Executive
Running QBRs without the exec sponsor for 2+ quarters. This signals declining strategic importance. If the exec stops attending, investigate: have they delegated permanently (acceptable if replacement has authority), or have they disengaged (Red flag requiring immediate relationship intervention)?

### The Vanity Metrics Show
Presenting usage counts, login numbers, and support ticket volumes without connecting them to business outcomes. Vanity metrics impress no one at the executive level. Translate every metric into business impact or remove it from the deck.

### The Avoided Conversation
Skipping difficult topics (declining usage, open escalations, competitor evaluation) hoping they resolve themselves. Address problems directly and early. Customers respect transparency. Avoiding hard conversations erodes trust faster than the problems themselves.

## QBR Effectiveness Scoring

Rate each QBR on these criteria (1-5 scale):

| Criterion | What 5 Looks Like | What 1 Looks Like |
|---|---|---|
| Customer talk time | Customer spoke 40%+ | Customer spoke <10% |
| Goal alignment | Clear objectives with metrics set | No objectives discussed |
| Exec engagement | Exec sponsor actively participated | Exec absent or disengaged |
| Value articulation | Quantified ROI presented | Feature usage stats only |
| Action clarity | All items have owners + deadlines | Vague commitments, no deadlines |
| Risk surfacing | Difficult topics addressed directly | Problems avoided or minimized |

Average score <3.0: redesign your QBR approach. Score 3.0-4.0: identify weakest criterion and improve. Score >4.0: strong QBR discipline.

## Virtual vs In-Person Considerations

| Factor | Virtual QBR | In-Person QBR |
|---|---|---|
| Best for | Mid-Market, routine quarterly check-ins | Enterprise, annual strategic reviews, relationship repair |
| Duration | Cap at 45 min (attention degrades after) | Up to 90 min (relationship building time included) |
| Preparation | Share pre-read deck 24h before | Bring printed executive summary |
| Engagement risk | Higher -- cameras off, multitasking | Lower -- social pressure maintains focus |
| Mitigation | Use polls, direct questions by name, shorter segments | Include informal time (lunch, coffee) for candid conversation |
| Follow-up | Same day email (urgency fades fast virtually) | Within 24h (in-person goodwill persists longer) |

Default to virtual for efficiency. Reserve in-person for: first QBR with new exec sponsor, accounts transitioning from Red to Yellow (relationship repair), annual strategic planning for top-tier accounts, and any account where the previous virtual QBR scored below 3.0.

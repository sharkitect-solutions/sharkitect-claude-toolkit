# Conversion & Revenue Marketing Ideas

Ideas focused on email, launches, promotions, pricing, referrals, and direct revenue impact.

---

## Email Marketing

| Idea | Description | When It Works | When It Fails | Effort | Timeline |
|------|-------------|---------------|---------------|--------|----------|
| Mistake Email Marketing | Send authentic "oops" emails when something genuinely goes wrong | Genuine mistake happened; transparency fits brand; audience is forgiving | Fake mistakes for engagement; too many "oops" emails; trivial issues | Low | Immediate |
| Reactivation Emails | Win back churned or inactive users with targeted campaigns | Clear reason to return (new features, fixed problems); enough churned users to segment | Spamming people who left for good reason; no new value to offer | Med | 1-3 months |
| Founder Welcome Email | Personal welcome from founder sharing story, asking about goals | Early-stage; founder has personality; creates genuine connection | Form letter pretending to be personal; founder too busy to actually reply | Low | Immediate |
| Dynamic Email Capture | Smart popups adapting to behavior (exit intent, scroll depth, time) | Significant traffic; clear offer; well-timed triggers | Popups on every page; no value exchange; annoying frequency | Med | 1-3 months |
| Monthly Newsletters | Consistent newsletters with curated news, insights, product updates | Something genuinely valuable to share monthly; consistent schedule | Newsletter is pure product updates; inconsistent publishing; no curation value | Med | 1-3 months |
| Inbox Placement | Technical email optimization (authentication, list hygiene, engagement signals) | Deliverability problems detected; list has grown without hygiene; authentication missing | Already at 95%+ deliverability; tiny list where individual problems dominate | Med | 1-3 months |
| Onboarding Emails | Behavior-triggered sequences guiding new users to activation | Clear activation metric; enough signups; behavior data available | Time-based drip with no personalization; emails about features not outcomes | Med | 1-3 months |
| Win-back Emails | Re-engage churned users with compelling reasons to return | Genuine improvements since they left; right timing; compelling offer | Begging emails; nothing has changed; sending too soon after churn | Med | 1-3 months |
| Trial Reactivation | Targeted campaigns to recover expired trials | New value since trial expired; right offer; enough expired trials to matter | Trial expired 6+ months ago; product unchanged; generic "come back" message | Med | 1-3 months |

## Launches & Promotions

| Idea | Description | When It Works | When It Fails | Effort | Timeline |
|------|-------------|---------------|---------------|--------|----------|
| Black Friday Promotions | Annual deals creating urgency and acquisition spikes | Product has annual pricing; audience expects BF deals; deal is genuinely good | Discounting to people who would pay full price; deal-seekers who churn in January | Med | 1-3 months |
| Product Hunt Launch | Structured PH launch reaching early adopters | Product is ready; launch day coordinated; community engaged in advance | Launching too early; no preparation; expecting PH alone to drive sustainable growth | High | 1-3 months |
| Early-Access Referrals | Waitlist referral programs (share to move up) | Pre-launch buzz exists; product has genuine demand; referral mechanic is clear | Nobody wants to wait; referral manipulation; product does not deliver on hype | Med | 1-3 months |
| New Year Promotions | Align promotions with fresh budgets and goal-setting energy | B2B with annual budgets; consumer products aligned with resolutions | Random discount with "New Year" slapped on it; no connection to renewal mindset | Med | Immediate |
| Early Access Pricing | Discounted pricing for early supporters who provide feedback | Pre-launch or early-stage; supporters accept rough edges for price; feedback is collected | Discount sets permanent price expectation; early users demand full support at discount price | Med | 1-3 months |
| Product Hunt Alternatives | Launch on BetaList, Launching Next, AlternativeTo, and similar platforms | Product ready; willing to launch multiple times; each platform has relevant audience | Same launch copy everywhere; platforms have no relevant traffic; launch fatigue | Low | 1-3 months |
| Twitter/X Giveaways | Engagement giveaways requiring follows, retweets, or tags | Strong X presence; prize is relevant to ICP (not generic); builds real following | Prize attracts bots; followers unfollow after giveaway; prize is your product nobody wants free | Low | Immediate |
| General Giveaways | Strategic giveaways attracting attention and capturing leads | Prize attracts ICP specifically; entry mechanic captures useful data; follow-up plan exists | Generic prize (iPad) attracting non-ICP; no conversion path post-giveaway | Med | Immediate |
| Vacation Giveaways | Grand prize (dream vacation) generating massive engagement | Huge audience reach goal; viral sharing mechanic; budget for prize; legal compliance | Expensive prize with zero qualified leads; legal headaches in multiple jurisdictions | High | 1-3 months |
| Lifetime Deals | One-time payment deals on AppSumo or similar platforms | Need cash injection; product has low marginal cost; can handle LTD users long-term | LTD users demand everything forever; revenue spike followed by support burden; devalues product | Med | 1-3 months |

## Referral & Audience-Specific

| Idea | Description | When It Works | When It Fails | Effort | Timeline |
|------|-------------|---------------|---------------|--------|----------|
| Two-Sided Referrals | Reward both referrer and referred user | Clear value for both sides; product has natural share moments; economics work | Referral incentive exceeds LTV; gaming the system; no natural share moment | Med | 1-3 months |
| Podcast Tours | Guest on multiple podcasts reaching your ICP | Interesting story to tell; relevant podcasts exist; good speaker | Boring guest; product pitch instead of value; podcasts with no listeners | Med | 3-6 months |
| Customer Language Mining | Extract exact words from reviews, support tickets, interviews | Enough customer communications to mine; language differs from your marketing copy | Already using customer language; too few data points; language is generic | Low | Immediate |

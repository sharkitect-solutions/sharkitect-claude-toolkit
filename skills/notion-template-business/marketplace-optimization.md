# Marketplace Optimization for Notion Templates

## Notion Marketplace Approval Process

The Notion Marketplace is curated -- not every template gets listed. Understanding the approval criteria prevents wasted submission attempts.

| Approval Criteria | What Reviewers Check | Common Rejection Reasons |
|---|---|---|
| Template quality | Clean layout, consistent styling, no broken links or empty pages | Placeholder text left in ("Lorem ipsum"), inconsistent icon usage, broken relation/rollup properties |
| Documentation | Getting started guide, feature descriptions | No onboarding flow. User opens template and doesn't know where to start |
| Uniqueness | Does this template add something the marketplace doesn't already have? | "Another habit tracker" -- too similar to 15 existing templates. Need a clear differentiator |
| Functionality | Template actually works as described. All formulas, rollups, and relations function correctly | Formulas that break when example data is deleted. Rollups referencing non-existent properties |
| Category fit | Template belongs in the category you selected | Submitting a "personal CRM" under "productivity" when "sales" is more accurate |

**Approval timeline**: 2-4 weeks for first submission. Resubmissions after rejection: 1-2 weeks. During high-volume periods (January, back-to-school): up to 6 weeks.

**Rejection recovery**: If rejected, you get specific feedback. Fix EXACTLY what they flagged -- don't assume other changes are needed. Resubmit with a note addressing each point. Second rejection on the same template = wait 30 days before trying again.

## Notion Marketplace Listing Optimization

| Element | Best Practice | Impact on Discovery | Common Mistake |
|---|---|---|---|
| Title | [Specific Audience] + [Core Function]. "Freelancer Client Portal" not "Ultimate Business Template" | HIGH -- title is the primary search match | Vague titles: "Life OS", "Second Brain 2.0", "The Ultimate System" -- these rank for nothing specific |
| Description (first 2 lines) | Problem statement + who it's for. Visible without clicking "Read more" | HIGH -- determines click-through from search results | Starting with features ("Includes 15 views, 8 databases...") instead of the problem solved |
| Preview images | Image 1: hero shot showing the template in use (not empty). Images 2-4: key features. Image 5: before/after or testimonial | HIGH -- visual quality determines click-through | Using screenshots of empty templates. Or using Figma mockups that look nothing like the actual template |
| Category selection | Pick the MOST specific category available. "Project Management" > "Productivity" | MEDIUM -- determines which browse lists you appear in | Choosing "Productivity" for everything. More specific categories have less competition |
| Tags | Use all available tag slots. Include use-case tags and audience tags | MEDIUM -- tags affect search matching | Generic tags: "notion", "template", "productivity". Use specific: "freelancer", "client-management", "invoicing" |

## Gumroad SEO and Discovery

Gumroad has its own search engine. Templates that rank well in Gumroad search get passive discovery traffic.

| Gumroad SEO Factor | How to Optimize | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Product title | Include primary keyword. "Notion Freelancer Dashboard - Client Portal & Invoice Tracker" | HIGH -- Gumroad search is title-weighted |
| Product description | Use headers (Gumroad supports markdown). Include keywords naturally in first paragraph | MEDIUM -- Gumroad indexes descriptions but title dominates |
| Tags | Gumroad allows up to 5 tags. Use: [audience] + [function] + [platform] + [problem] + [format] | MEDIUM -- tags drive category placement and related product suggestions |
| Thumbnail | 1280x720px minimum. Show the template, not just text. Gumroad crops thumbnails differently on mobile vs desktop | HIGH -- thumbnail is the ONLY visual in search results. Test both crops |
| Reviews | First 10 reviews are critical. Send follow-up email 3 days after purchase asking for review. Offer bonus content for reviews (ethical, not pay-for-review) | VERY HIGH -- Gumroad sorts by rating + review count. 4.8 stars with 50 reviews beats 5.0 with 3 reviews |
| Price | Gumroad's "Discover" section favors products between $9-49. Premium products ($100+) appear less in browse | MEDIUM -- affects algorithmic placement, not search ranking |

## Lemon Squeezy Optimization

| Feature | How to Use for Templates | Gotcha |
|---|---|---|
| Digital delivery | Upload .zip with Notion duplicate link + documentation PDF | Lemon Squeezy's download page is cleaner than Gumroad's. Reduces "where's my template?" support emails |
| Tax handling | Lemon Squeezy handles EU VAT, US sales tax automatically | If selling internationally, this saves you from VAT registration nightmares. Main advantage over Gumroad for EU sellers |
| License keys | Can generate unique license keys per purchase | Useful for B2B templates where you want to track installations. Overkill for consumer templates |
| Discount codes | Create unlimited discount codes with usage limits | Use for: launch discounts (72h timer), bundle upsells ("add the Pro version for 30% off"), affiliate partners |
| Checkout overlay | Embed checkout on your own site without redirect | Keeps users on your site. Higher conversion than redirect to Gumroad (10-20% improvement in some tests) |

## Cross-Platform Listing Strategy

| Strategy | When to Use | Revenue Split | Management Overhead |
|---|---|---|---|
| Same template everywhere | Simple templates under $29 | Even split across platforms | LOW -- same listing, same price, same assets |
| Tiered by platform | Templates with clear free/pro split | Free on Notion Marketplace, paid on Gumroad/LS | MEDIUM -- maintain 2 versions, but free version drives paid sales |
| Exclusive by platform | Premium templates $50+ | 100% direct (own site + Stripe/LS) | LOW -- one listing, one channel, full margin |
| Marketplace for discovery, direct for revenue | Any template with marketing funnel | 80%+ from direct, marketplace for social proof | HIGH -- different pricing, need cross-linking, manage two customer experiences |

**The platform arbitrage play**: List a "lite" version on Notion Marketplace (free or $9). Include a link to the "Pro version" on your own site ($49-99). Marketplace gives you free discovery. Your site gives you margin and email addresses. This is the most profitable strategy for most template creators.

## Listing Optimization Checklist

Run this before every new listing:

- [ ] Title contains primary keyword AND target audience
- [ ] First 2 lines of description state the problem, not features
- [ ] At least 4 preview images showing template WITH data (not empty)
- [ ] Video walkthrough linked (Loom, YouTube, or native embed)
- [ ] Pricing includes at least 2 tiers (basic + pro)
- [ ] Tags use all available slots with specific keywords
- [ ] Getting started documentation is inside the template itself
- [ ] Mobile preview tested (many marketplaces display poorly on mobile)
- [ ] Competitive analysis done: checked top 5 similar templates for pricing and positioning
- [ ] First review strategy planned (who will you ask for the first 5-10 reviews?)

# Follow-Up Sequences

## Why Follow-Ups Matter

Over half of replies stem from follow-ups rather than initial outreach. Campaigns of 4-7 emails achieve 27% reply rates vs 9% for 1-3 emails.

## Recommended Cadence

Space messages progressively further apart:

- **Day 0:** Initial contact
- **Day 3:** First follow-up (can boost response by up to 31%)
- **Days 7-8:** Second follow-up with different angle
- **Day 14:** Third follow-up introducing new value
- **Days 21-28:** Final breakup email

Send during Tuesday-Thursday windows, preferring morning or early afternoon hours in the prospect's timezone.

## Content Strategy

Each message requires a distinct angle:

| Touch | Angle |
|-------|-------|
| Initial | Core value proposition |
| FU1 | Fresh statistic or industry insight |
| FU2 | Case study or social proof |
| FU3 | Different angle or resource |
| FU4 (Breakup) | Clean exit, loss aversion trigger |

Avoid generic phrases like "just checking in" or "bumping this to the top of your inbox" -- these undermine credibility.

## The Breakup Email

This final touch acknowledges silence while leaving dialogue open. It works by triggering loss aversion. Offering a simple response format (like "1-2-3 choose your option") reduces friction and reportedly generates 10-15% response rates.

**Critical commitment:** Once you send a breakup email, respect that boundary and cease outreach.

## Angle Rotation Examples

**Initial:** Problem-focused (their pain)
**FU1:** Data-focused (industry stat that validates the pain)
**FU2:** Proof-focused (case study or result)
**FU3:** Resource-focused (share something useful even if they don't reply)
**FU4:** Exit-focused (respect their time, leave door open)

## Key Principles

1. Each email should stand alone (they may not have read previous ones)
2. Never reference "my last email" or guilt them for not responding
3. Add new value with every touch -- different angle, fresh proof, useful insight
4. Increasing gaps between emails shows respect for their time
5. The breakup email is sacred -- honor it and stop

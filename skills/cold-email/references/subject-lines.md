# Subject Line Optimization

## Key Findings

**Length matters significantly.** 2-word subject lines get 60% more opens than 5-word versions. Keep to 2-4 words consistently.

**Appearance trumps cleverness.** Subject lines designed to resemble internal communications double open rates by avoiding the appearance of marketing material. Examples: "reply rates," "trial delays," "Q2 forecast."

**Lowercase performs best.** Across 85+ million emails analyzed, all-lowercase formatting achieved the highest open rates because it looks more personal/internal.

**Personalization requires strategy.** Contextual details boost engagement by 26-50%, but using first names actually signals automation and reduces replies by 12%. Focus on specific pain points or competitive references instead.

## What Works

- 2-4 words, lowercase
- Looks like an internal message
- References their world (industry, challenge, role)
- Creates curiosity without being clickbaity
- No punctuation tricks, no caps, no emojis

## Examples by Type

**Internal-looking:** "scheduling gaps," "response times," "Q1 pipeline"
**Challenge-based:** "scaling bottleneck," "lead follow-up"
**Curiosity:** "quick question," "noticed something"

## What to Avoid

- Salesy language (reduces opens by 17.9%)
- Numbers/percentages (drop performance by 46%)
- Emojis (harm B2B credibility)
- Generic questions (underperform vs. specific challenges)
- Prospect's first name (signals automation)
- ALL CAPS or Title Case (looks like marketing)
- "Re:" or "Fwd:" tricks (destroys trust)

## Executive Targeting

C-suite recipients respond 23% more frequently when emails avoid sales language entirely. Use ultra-concise, human, understated approaches referencing specific initiatives or events.

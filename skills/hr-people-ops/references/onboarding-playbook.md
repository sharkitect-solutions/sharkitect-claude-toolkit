# Onboarding Playbook Reference

## Pre-Boarding Checklist (Offer Accepted to Day 1)

Complete all items at least 3 business days before the start date. Pre-boarding failures create a chaotic Day 1 that permanently colors the new hire's perception of the company.

### IT and Equipment
- [ ] Order laptop/workstation (configured with standard software image)
- [ ] Create email account and add to team distribution lists
- [ ] Provision access: Slack/Teams, project management tool, code repos, CRM, shared drives
- [ ] Set up phone/extension (if applicable)
- [ ] Ship equipment to home address (for remote hires) with tracking number shared

### HR and Admin
- [ ] Send offer letter and collect signed copy
- [ ] Send new hire paperwork: I-9, W-4, direct deposit, emergency contact, benefits enrollment
- [ ] Add to payroll system with correct start date, salary, and classification
- [ ] Schedule Day 1 orientation and first-week meetings on their calendar
- [ ] Send welcome packet: company handbook, team directory, org chart, parking/office access info

### Team Preparation
- [ ] Assign an onboarding buddy (peer-level, not the manager). The buddy answers "dumb questions" the new hire won't ask the manager.
- [ ] Brief the team: new hire's name, role, start date, background summary (with permission)
- [ ] Prepare a first small win assignment -- a real task (not busywork) completable in 2-3 days
- [ ] Manager prepares a written 30-60-90 day plan tailored to the role

## Day 1 Agenda Template

**Morning (9:00-12:00)**

| Time | Activity | Owner |
|------|----------|-------|
| 9:00-9:30 | Welcome and office tour (or virtual workspace tour) | Manager |
| 9:30-10:00 | IT setup: verify all accounts, tools, and access working | IT / Buddy |
| 10:00-10:45 | Company orientation: mission, values, product overview, business model | Manager or founder (at SMBs, founder involvement signals importance) |
| 10:45-11:00 | Break |  |
| 11:00-11:30 | Team introductions: each team member shares name, role, and one current project | Team |
| 11:30-12:00 | Buddy lunch scheduling, Slack channel introductions, informal questions | Buddy |

**Afternoon (1:00-5:00)**

| Time | Activity | Owner |
|------|----------|-------|
| 1:00-2:00 | Tools and systems walkthrough: how the team communicates, where docs live, how work gets tracked | Buddy |
| 2:00-2:30 | Review 30-60-90 day plan together. Clarify expectations, ask questions. | Manager |
| 2:30-3:30 | First small win assignment: hand off a real, bounded task with clear success criteria | Manager |
| 3:30-4:00 | Compliance training kickoff: schedule remaining sessions for Week 1 | HR |
| 4:00-4:30 | End-of-day check-in: "How did today go? Any questions? Anything you need?" | Manager |

**The first small win matters.** It should be a genuine contribution -- fixing a small bug, drafting a social post, organizing a dataset, responding to a support ticket. Completing real work on Day 1 builds confidence and signals belonging. Busywork ("read these 40 docs") signals the company is not ready for the new hire.

## Week 1 Structure

| Day | Focus | Key Activities |
|-----|-------|---------------|
| Day 1 | Orientation and setup | See Day 1 agenda above |
| Day 2 | Shadow and learn | Shadow a team member through their workflow. Attend a team meeting as observer. Review key documentation (product specs, process docs, team wiki). |
| Day 3 | Stakeholder connections | Meet 2-3 cross-functional partners (people the role interacts with regularly). Understand how their work connects to others. |
| Day 4 | Deepen and contribute | Continue first small win. Start second task. Begin compliance training modules. |
| Day 5 | Week 1 retro | Complete first small win. End-of-week check-in with manager: "What makes sense? What's confusing? What do you need more of?" |

### Compliance Training (Complete by End of Week 2)
Sexual harassment prevention, data security/privacy, workplace safety (OSHA basics), code of conduct acknowledgment, and benefits enrollment deadline reminder (typically 30 days from start).

## 30-Day Milestone

**Target state:** The new hire understands their role clearly, has delivered 2-3 meaningful contributions, and can navigate daily workflows independently.

### Assessment Checklist (Manager and New Hire Complete Together)
- [ ] Role clarity: "Can you describe your top 3 responsibilities and how success is measured?" (The new hire should articulate this without referencing the job description)
- [ ] First deliverable completed and reviewed with feedback
- [ ] Key relationships established: knows who to go to for what
- [ ] Tools proficiency: can use all daily tools without assistance
- [ ] Team norms understood: meeting cadence, communication preferences, decision-making process

### 30-Day Check-In Questions (Manager Asks)
1. "What has surprised you most about the role or team?"
2. "What's working well in your onboarding?"
3. "What's one thing you'd change about how we brought you in?"
4. "Is anything unclear about expectations or priorities?"
5. "Do you have everything you need to be successful?"

**Red flag at 30 days:** The new hire cannot articulate their role's priorities or has not completed any independent work. Investigate immediately -- the problem is usually unclear expectations (manager's responsibility), not capability.

## 60-Day Milestone

**Target state:** Independent contribution. Identifies problems and proposes solutions rather than waiting for direction.

**Assessment:** Independent task completion without step-by-step guidance. Working rapport with cross-functional partners. Raises blockers early. Demonstrates improvement from prior feedback. Participates in team rituals.

**Bidirectional feedback session:** Manager provides SBI-model feedback (see performance-framework.md). New hire provides feedback on onboarding and management. If serious fit or performance concerns exist, the 60-day mark is the decision point -- do not wait until 90 days.

## 90-Day Milestone

**Target state:** Full productivity for level. Understands business context, not just tasks.

**Assessment:** Output quality and volume match role expectations. Can explain how work connects to company goals. Built trust with stakeholders beyond immediate team. Identified 1-2 growth areas. First formal performance check-in completed.

**Conversation framework:** Review original 30-60-90 plan (what completed, what shifted). Discuss strengths and development areas. Co-create 6-month development plan. Ask: "Knowing what you know now, would you take this job again?" Set quarterly goals (transition to standard performance cycle).

## Onboarding Metrics

| Metric | Target | How to Measure | When to Measure |
|--------|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Time-to-productivity | Role-dependent: 30 days (junior), 60 days (mid), 90 days (senior) | Manager assessment against role-specific output benchmarks | 30/60/90 day milestones |
| 90-day retention rate | Above 90% | New hires still employed at 90 days / total new hires | Rolling quarterly |
| New hire NPS | Above 60 | Survey at 30 and 90 days: "On 0-10, how likely are you to recommend this company as a great place to work?" | 30 and 90 days |
| Onboarding completion rate | 100% | All checklist items completed by specified deadlines | Weekly tracking during first 90 days |
| Manager satisfaction | Above 4/5 | Manager rates onboarding process effectiveness | 90-day review |

## Remote Onboarding Adaptations

Remote onboarding requires 30-50% more structure. Informal learning does not happen organically -- replace it deliberately.

- Ship equipment 5+ business days early (not 3). Schedule a "tech test" call the day before start.
- Increase buddy check-ins to daily (15 min) for first two weeks, then taper to 2x/week.
- Schedule virtual coffee chats with 3-4 team members in Week 1 (15 min each, no agenda -- purely social).
- Use video for all onboarding meetings. Create an async onboarding channel for questions.
- Manager check-ins: daily for Week 1, 3x/week for Weeks 2-4, then standard 1:1 cadence.

## Manager's Onboarding Checklist (Separate from HR's)

The manager owns the new hire's success. HR provides infrastructure; the manager provides context, direction, and belonging.

| Phase | Manager Actions |
|-------|----------------|
| Before Day 1 | Write 30-60-90 plan with deliverables. Identify and brief buddy. Prepare first small win. Block calendar for daily Week 1 check-ins. |
| Week 1 | Conduct Day 1 welcome. Introduce new hire with context. Walk through 30-60-90 plan. Assign first small win with success criteria. End-of-week retro. |
| Month 1 | Weekly 1:1s focused on onboarding progress. Actionable feedback on first deliverables. Connect with cross-functional stakeholders. 30-day milestone review. |
| Months 2-3 | Transition to standard management cadence. 60-day bidirectional feedback. 90-day formal check-in and development plan. Confirm quarterly review cycle. |

## Common Onboarding Failures and Fixes

| Failure | Root Cause | Fix |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| New hire sits idle for 2 days | No pre-boarding, no first assignment | Complete pre-boarding checklist. Assign first small win before Day 1. |
| New hire feels isolated (remote) | No buddy, no social connections | Assign buddy Day 1. Schedule coffee chats. Daily check-ins Week 1. |
| Information overload | 40-page handbook dumped on Day 1 | Spread across 4 weeks with context: "Read this BECAUSE you need it for X." |
| Unclear priorities at 30 days | No written plan, no expectation-setting | Written 30-60-90 plan co-reviewed Day 1. Weekly alignment check-ins. |
| Leaves within 90 days | Role misaligned with job posting | Fix upstream: accurate JDs, realistic previews, Day 1 scope confirmation. |

## Onboarding by Role Type

| Role Type | Timeline | Key Additions |
|-----------|----------|---------------|
| Individual Contributor | Standard 30-60-90 | Technical ramp-up, tool proficiency, team integration. First deliverable by Week 2. |
| Manager | 120 days to full effectiveness | Skip-levels with each report (Wk 1-2), team dynamics assessment (Mo 1), first team retro (Mo 2), first performance conversations (Mo 3). No structural changes in first 60 days. |
| Executive | 6-month horizon | Board/investor intros (Wk 1-2), strategic presentation (Mo 2), first QBR (Mo 3). Executive peer as onboarding partner. CEO weekly 1:1s for 90 days. |

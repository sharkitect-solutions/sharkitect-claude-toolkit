# Hiring Pipeline Reference

## Sourcing Strategy by Channel

| Channel | Best For | Cost | Time-to-Hire | Quality Signal |
|---------|----------|------|-------------|----------------|
| Job boards (Indeed, LinkedIn Jobs) | Volume roles, broad reach | $200-500/posting | 30-45 days | Medium -- high volume, variable quality |
| Employee referrals | Mid-senior roles, culture-aligned candidates | $1,000-5,000 bonus | 15-25 days | High -- pre-vetted by trusted source |
| LinkedIn Recruiter (direct outreach) | Passive candidates, specialized roles | $8,000-12,000/year | 20-35 days | High -- targeted, but lower response rate (15-25%) |
| Recruiting agencies | Executive roles, urgent fills, niche skills | 15-25% of first-year salary | 30-60 days | High -- but expensive. Use when internal sourcing fails after 3 weeks |
| Community sourcing (Slack groups, meetups, niche forums) | Technical and creative roles | Free-$500 | 20-40 days | Very high -- self-selected community members |

### Referral Program Design

Offer tiered bonuses: $1,000 for individual contributors, $2,500 for senior roles, $5,000 for director+. Pay 50% at hire, 50% at 90-day retention mark. Cap referral hires at 40% of total pipeline to prevent homogeneity. Track referral source diversity quarterly.

## Job Posting Optimization

**Title SEO:** Use the title candidates actually search for, not your internal title. "Marketing Manager" outperforms "Growth Ninja." Include seniority level. "Senior Software Engineer" gets 3x the views of "Software Engineer III."

**Salary transparency:** Listings with salary ranges receive 30% more applicants. States with pay transparency laws (CA, CO, NY, WA) require it. Include ranges even where not required -- it filters out misaligned candidates early, saving 5-10 hours of wasted screening per role.

**Application friction:** Every additional required field reduces completion rate by 5-10%. Require: resume, optional cover letter, 1-2 screening questions. Do not require: separate address fields, references upfront, lengthy custom forms. Target under 5 minutes to apply.

## Phone Screen Template (15 Minutes)

1. **Role fit (3 min):** "What attracted you to this role? Walk me through your understanding of what this position does day to day."
2. **Experience snapshot (5 min):** "Tell me about your most recent role. What were you responsible for, and what did you achieve?"
3. **Compensation alignment (3 min):** "The range for this role is $X-$Y. Does that align with your expectations?" -- Surface misalignment now, not after 3 interviews.
4. **Logistics (2 min):** Start date availability, location/remote confirmation, visa status (if applicable).
5. **Questions (2 min):** "What questions do you have about the role or company?"

**Decision:** Pass/fail only. Do not score. The phone screen is a logistics and alignment filter.

## Interview Loop Design by Role Type

**Individual Contributor (3-4 interviews):**
1. Hiring manager: role fit, behavioral competencies (45 min)
2. Peer: technical/functional assessment (45-60 min)
3. Cross-functional partner: collaboration and communication (30 min)
4. Optional: work sample review or live exercise (60 min)

**Manager (4-5 interviews):**
1. Hiring manager (skip-level): leadership philosophy, strategic thinking (45 min)
2. Peer manager: cross-team collaboration, conflict resolution (30 min)
3. Future direct report(s): management style, coaching approach (30 min)
4. HR/People partner: people management track record, difficult conversations (30 min)
5. Work sample: present a 90-day plan for the team they would manage

**Executive (5-6 interviews + board/investor meeting):**
1. CEO: vision alignment, strategic capability (60 min)
2. Peer executives: cross-functional leadership (30 min each, 2-3 peers)
3. Board member or investor (if applicable): governance and accountability (30 min)
4. Direct reports panel: leadership assessment (45 min)
5. Case study or strategic presentation (60 min)

## Behavioral Interview Questions (STAR Format)

Map each question to a specific competency. Use the same questions for every candidate for the same role.

| Competency | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| Problem-solving | "Describe a time you identified a problem before anyone else noticed it. What did you do?" |
| Ownership | "Tell me about a project that failed. What was your role, and what would you do differently?" |
| Collaboration | "Give an example of a time you had to work with someone whose style was very different from yours. How did you handle it?" |
| Execution under pressure | "Describe a situation where you had to deliver a result with an immovable deadline and insufficient resources." |
| Growth mindset | "Tell me about the most significant piece of critical feedback you've received. How did you respond?" |
| Leadership (for managers) | "Describe a time you had to make an unpopular decision with your team. How did you communicate it?" |

## Technical Assessment Principles

**Take-home assignments:** Cap at 2-4 hours. Define the scope clearly. Provide a rubric in advance. Compensate candidates ($200-500) for assignments exceeding 2 hours. Grade against the rubric, not against other candidates. Blind-grade when possible (remove candidate names).

**Live coding/system design:** Allocate 45-60 minutes. Allow the candidate to use their own tools (IDE, browser, documentation). Evaluate problem-solving approach, communication, and code quality -- not memorization of syntax or algorithms.

**Portfolio review:** Request 2-3 work samples relevant to the role. Discuss decisions, tradeoffs, and outcomes during the interview. Evaluate judgment and craft, not volume.

## Debrief Process

1. All interviewers submit written scorecards within 4 hours of their interview. No exceptions.
2. Debrief meeting opens with silent scorecard review (5 min). Every participant reads all scorecards before discussion begins.
3. Each interviewer shares their hire/no-hire recommendation with supporting evidence. Start with the most junior interviewer to prevent anchoring by senior voices.
4. Discuss disagreements. Focus on evidence, not opinions.
5. Hiring manager makes the final call. If the decision is not unanimous, the hiring manager must document the dissenting view and their reasoning for overriding it.

**Bias mitigation:** Ban phrases like "gut feeling," "not sure why, but," and "something about them." Every objection requires a specific behavioral example from the interview.

## Offer Construction

| Lever | When to Use |
|-------|------------|
| Higher base | Candidate has competing offers or is at the top of your band |
| Equity (startups) | Candidate values long-term upside; use to bridge base salary gaps |
| Signing bonus | Candidate is leaving unvested equity or a bonus cycle; one-time bridge |
| Accelerated review | Candidate is below band midpoint; promise a 6-month comp review with specific criteria |
| Title adjustment | Only if the title accurately reflects the scope. Never inflate titles as a compensation substitute. |
| Remote/flexibility | High-value, low-cost lever. Offering 1-2 remote days can offset $5-10K in base salary expectations. |

## Offer Negotiation Playbook (Employer Side)

1. Open with your best reasonable offer, not a lowball. Starting low and "negotiating up" signals distrust.
2. When the candidate counters: ask "What would make this a clear yes for you?" Understand their priorities before adjusting numbers.
3. Never negotiate against yourself. If the candidate counters, make one adjustment and hold. "This is the best we can do within our band for this level."
4. Set a decision deadline: 3-5 business days. Generous but bounded. Open-ended offers get shopped.
5. If the candidate declines: ask what changed. The answer improves your next offer to the next candidate.

## Reference Check Template (5 Questions)

1. "What was your working relationship with [candidate]? How long did you work together?"
2. "What were [candidate]'s greatest strengths in the role?"
3. "If you could suggest one area for [candidate] to develop, what would it be?"
4. "On a scale of 1-10, how likely would you be to hire [candidate] again? What would make it a 10?"
5. "Is there anything else I should know that would help [candidate] succeed in this role?"

**Red flag:** A reference who cannot answer question 4 with a 7+ is signaling a problem. A reference who hesitates on "would you hire them again" is giving you more information than their words.

## Candidate Experience Metrics

| Metric | Target | How to Measure |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Time-to-hire (posting to accepted offer) | Under 35 days for IC, under 50 days for management | ATS tracking |
| Offer acceptance rate | Above 85% | Offers extended vs accepted |
| Candidate NPS | Above 50 | Post-process survey (send to all finalists, including rejected) |
| Interview-to-offer ratio | 3:1 or better | Candidates interviewed vs offers extended |
| Source quality ratio | Track by channel | Hires per channel / applicants per channel |

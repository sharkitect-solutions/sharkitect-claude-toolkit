# Multi-Format Editing Guide

Load when editing copy across different formats (landing pages, emails, ads, product descriptions, social media) where each format has specific constraints that modify how the Seven Sweeps apply.

## Format-Specific Sweep Modifications

### Landing Pages

| Sweep | Landing Page Adaptation | Why Different |
|---|---|---|
| S1 Clarity | Above-fold headline must communicate value in <8 words. Subheadline adds specificity. | Visitors decide to stay or leave in 3-5 seconds. Below fold can be longer. |
| S3 So What | Every section needs its own benefit statement, not just features | Long-form pages lose readers at each section break. "So what" bridges keep them scrolling. |
| S4 Prove It | Place strongest proof within 2 scrolls of each CTA | Proof decays with distance from the action point. Don't cluster all testimonials in one section. |
| S5 Specificity | Headline = specific outcome. Body = how you get there. | Specific headlines convert 2-3x better than vague ones on landing pages specifically. |
| S7 Zero Risk | Money-back guarantee, free trial, or "cancel anytime" within visual proximity of CTA button | The CTA is the highest-friction moment. Risk reversal at that exact point reduces abandonment. |

**Landing page structure rule**: Hero (what + for whom) -> Problem (why it matters) -> Solution (how it works) -> Proof (why trust us) -> CTA (what to do next). Edit to reinforce this flow, not to rearrange it.

### Email Sequences

| Sweep | Email Adaptation | Why Different |
|---|---|---|
| S1 Clarity | Subject line = one clear idea in <50 characters (mobile preview cuts at 35-40 chars on most devices) | Subject line is the ONLY copy most recipients see. Clarity here determines open rate. |
| S2 Voice | Each email in a sequence must sound like the same person. Check by reading email 1 then email 5 -- voice drift is cumulative. | Sequence emails are written at different times, often by different people. Voice drift is the #1 sequence quality issue. |
| S3 So What | One email = one ask. If the email contains two CTAs, split into two emails. | Email engagement drops 50%+ when multiple CTAs compete (Wordstream study). |
| S6 Emotion | First line after subject line carries all the emotional weight. If it doesn't hook, they close. | Preview text (first 90-140 characters) shows in the inbox. It functions as a second headline. |
| S7 Zero Risk | Unsubscribe link must be visible and functional. "Reply to this email" as CTA reduces friction vs. clicking a link. | Email has the lowest commitment threshold. Removing even small friction points has outsized impact. |

**Email editing sequence**: Edit subject lines last, after body copy is finalized. Subject lines should reflect the strongest element in the body, which you won't know until the body is edited.

**Preview text trap**: Many email builders auto-populate preview text from the first line of body copy. If the first line is "Hi [Name]," the preview is wasted. Set explicit preview text that extends the subject line's promise.

### Ad Copy (Google, Meta, LinkedIn)

| Sweep | Ad Adaptation | Constraint |
|---|---|---|
| S1 Clarity | Message must be complete in the character limit. No "learn more to find out..." | Google RSA headlines: 30 chars. Descriptions: 90 chars. Meta primary text: 125 chars visible before "See More" |
| S3 So What | Lead with benefit, not feature. Feature goes in description or sitelink. | Headlines with benefits get 28% higher CTR than feature-based headlines (Google Ads benchmark) |
| S4 Prove It | Numbers and social proof in headlines outperform claims | "Join 12,000+ teams" or "4.9/5 on G2" -- concrete proof in tiny character counts |
| S5 Specificity | Every word must earn its place. Remove articles ("a," "the"), filler ("great," "best"), and implied words. | "Save 4h/week on reports" not "Save a great deal of time on your weekly reports" |

**Ad editing rule**: Write the ad at 2x the character limit, then cut by half. Cutting forces you to keep only the strongest words. Writing to the limit produces bloated copy.

| Platform | Headline Limit | Description Limit | Key Constraint |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google RSA | 30 chars x 15 | 90 chars x 4 | Headlines must work in any combination -- no sequential dependency |
| Meta (Facebook/Instagram) | 40 chars recommended | 125 chars visible + "See More" | Primary text before "See More" must contain the complete hook |
| LinkedIn Sponsored | 70 chars | 150 chars intro | Professional tone mandatory -- casual copy underperforms by 30% on LinkedIn |
| Microsoft Ads | 30 chars x 15 | 90 chars x 4 | Same as Google RSA -- import with adaptation, not direct copy |

### Product Descriptions (E-commerce)

| Sweep | Product Description Adaptation | Why Different |
|---|---|---|
| S1 Clarity | Lead with what the product IS, not what it does. Shoppers need category recognition first. | "Wireless noise-canceling headphones" before "Block out the world and focus" -- categorize then sell |
| S3 So What | Benefits must relate to use context, not abstract improvements | "Lasts through a full transatlantic flight on one charge" not "40-hour battery life" |
| S4 Prove It | Award badges, review counts, and expert endorsements outperform written testimonials on product pages | Shoppers scan product pages -- visual proof (badges, stars, logos) registers faster than text proof |
| S5 Specificity | Materials, dimensions, compatibility, and "what's in the box" are conversion drivers, not filler | 30% of e-commerce returns cite "not what I expected" -- specifics prevent returns (Narvar 2023) |
| S6 Emotion | Sensory language for the "unboxing moment" -- what does it feel, look, smell like? | Online shoppers can't touch products. Sensory language bridges the physical gap. |

**Bullet point rule**: Product page bullets follow the pattern: [Benefit]: [Feature that enables it]. "Never miss a call: tri-band antenna connects in basements, elevators, and rural areas." Not: "Tri-band antenna for superior connectivity."

### Social Media Posts

| Sweep | Social Adaptation | Why Different |
|---|---|---|
| S1 Clarity | First line must stand alone as a complete thought. If the user doesn't tap "...more," you had one line to communicate. | Twitter/X: 280 chars total. LinkedIn: first ~210 chars visible before "...see more." Instagram: first 125 chars visible. |
| S2 Voice | Match platform culture. LinkedIn ≠ Twitter ≠ Instagram. | Same message, different packaging. LinkedIn = professional insight. Twitter = sharp take. Instagram = visual story with caption support. |
| S3 So What | The "so what" IS the post. No buildup, no context-setting. | Social audiences scroll at 1.7 seconds per post (Facebook internal data). Hook or die. |
| S5 Specificity | One specific detail beats three general points | "We shipped 14 features in 11 days" not "We've been busy shipping lots of updates" |

**Social editing trap**: Don't edit social posts the same way you edit long-form copy. Social is conversation, not persuasion. The goal is engagement (comments, shares), not conversion. Edit for "would someone reply to this?" not "does this sell?"

## Cross-Format Consistency Editing

When editing copy across multiple formats for the same campaign:

| Check | How | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Core message alignment | Extract the one-sentence value proposition from each format. They must match. | Ad says "Save 4 hours/week," landing page says "Boost productivity," email says "Work smarter" = three different promises for one product |
| Tone consistency | Read ad -> landing page -> email in sequence. Does it feel like the same brand? | Tone shifts between touchpoints create cognitive dissonance that reduces trust |
| Promise escalation, not contradiction | Each format should deepen the same promise, not make new ones | Ad: "Cut reporting time in half." Landing page: "From 8 hours to 45 minutes of reporting, every week." Email: "Here's how [Company] went from 8-hour reports to a 45-minute automated dashboard." |
| CTA progression | Ad CTA -> Landing page CTA -> Email CTA should follow commitment escalation | Ad: "See how" (low commitment). Landing page: "Start free trial" (medium). Email: "Book a demo with your data" (high). |

## Character Count Quick Reference

| Format | Element | Limit | Visible Without Interaction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google RSA | Headline | 30 chars | Yes (up to 3 shown) |
| Google RSA | Description | 90 chars | Yes (up to 2 shown) |
| Meta Primary Text | Body | 63,206 chars | First 125 chars |
| LinkedIn Post | Body | 3,000 chars | First ~210 chars |
| Twitter/X | Post | 280 chars | All |
| Email | Subject line | No hard limit | 35-50 chars on mobile |
| Email | Preview text | No hard limit | 90-140 chars on mobile |
| SMS | Message | 160 chars/segment | All (but billed per segment) |
| Push notification | Body | 178 chars (iOS) / 65 chars (Android visible) | Varies by device |

**Editing for truncation**: Always edit the visible portion first. If the copy is truncated by the platform, the visible part must contain the complete hook, benefit, and reason to expand.
